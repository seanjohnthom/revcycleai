UnitedHealthcare Expands Prior Auth for Outpatient Procedures: The March 1 Deadline

New requirements hit additional outpatient surgical procedures and imaging. Check which codes are affected and update your workflow now.

Source: RevCycle Intelligence February 18, 2026 7 min read

UnitedHealthcare is adding prior authorization requirements for a new set of outpatient surgical procedures and advanced imaging, effective March 1, 2026. If your team bills UHC commercial or Medicare Advantage and performs any of the affected procedures, claims submitted without authorization on or after March 1 will deny.

This is one of the more consequential PA expansions UHC has made in the last two years â€” the affected code categories include high-volume outpatient surgery and imaging that many practices have been billing without PA for years.

What's Changing

UHC is expanding the prior authorization list in three primary categories:

  • Outpatient surgical procedures â€” additional musculoskeletal, ENT, and GI procedures now require PA when performed in an ASC or outpatient hospital setting
  • Advanced imaging â€” expanded PA requirements for MRI, CT, and PET scans, including some combinations that were previously exempt
  • Sleep studies â€” new PA requirements for home sleep testing in certain plan types

March 1, 2026 for UHC commercial plans. Medicare Advantage plans may have a staggered rollout â€” confirm the effective date for each MA plan ID separately via the UHC provider portal.

Who's Most Affected

The expansion hits orthopedic, ENT, and GI specialties hardest. ASC operators billing UHC should treat this as a priority â€” the outpatient surgical PA expansion is the most operationally disruptive change because these are scheduled procedures with hard appointment dates. A denied authorization three days before surgery is a different problem than a denied outpatient claim.

Radiology and imaging centers are also in the crosshairs on the imaging expansion. If your group reads high volumes of spine MRIs or abdominal CTs for UHC members, you need to verify whether your ordering physicians are submitting PA requests â€” or whether your front desk is flagging UHC members before the order is finalized.

What UHC Is Not Changing

Routine office visits, preventive care, and most E&M services are not affected by this expansion. Emergency and urgent care services are exempt from PA requirements as usual. Lab services and most DMEPOS items are also not part of this update.

The operational challenge isn't knowing about the change â€” it's the 10-day window between now and March 1. Here's the specific problem: any procedure scheduled for March 1 or later that's currently booked without a PA needs to either get authorized before the visit or get rescheduled until authorization is in place.

  2. Submit PA requests now for any affected procedure scheduled on or after March 1
  3. Update your scheduling system to flag UHC patients for PA check on all affected code categories
